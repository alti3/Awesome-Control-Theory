# State-Space Representation

The most powerful and general way to represent linear systems.

$$
\dot{x}(t) = Ax(t) + Bu(t)
$$

$$
y(t) = Cx(t) + Du(t)
$$

Where:

- $x \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is the **state vector**
- $u \in \mathbb{R}^m$ is the **input**
- $y \in \mathbb{R}^p$ is the **output**

## Controllability

A system is controllable if the controllability matrix has full rank:

$$
\mathcal{C} = [B \quad AB \quad A^2B \quad \dots \quad A^{n-1}B]
$$
