## What It Means

**Outlier rejection** is an sensor fusion topic in the **State Estimation** branch of the control-theory map. In this context, it focuses on combining redundant or complementary measurements into a consistent estimate. It belongs to estimation methods that infer unmeasured states, parameters, disturbances, maps, or tracks from models and data, so the important question is not only what the method computes, but what assumptions make the computation meaningful for a closed loop.

A useful way to read this topic is as a connection between three objects: a model of the plant or signal, an objective stated in engineering terms, and evidence that the resulting behavior is stable, accurate, robust, or safe enough for the application.

## Mathematical Form

The estimator balances model prediction and measurement correction:

$$\hat x_k^+=\hat x_k^-+K_k\bigl(y_k-C\hat x_k^-\bigr).$$

The notation should be interpreted with the page topic in mind: $x$ is the state or configuration, $u$ is the manipulated input, $y$ is the measured output, $r$ is a reference, $e$ is an error signal, and disturbances or modeling errors are included when they materially affect the design.

## Intuition

The key intuition is to ask what information this topic adds to the feedback loop. Some topics add a model, some add a controller, some add an estimator, and some add a test. For **Outlier rejection**, the central issue is the behavior summarized above: fault detection prevents bad measurements from corrupting estimates. The method still has to remain consistent with the plant assumptions and the performance objective.

A practical example is vehicle navigation: an inertial model predicts motion between updates, while GPS, camera, wheel, or range measurements correct drift and expose outliers through residuals.

## Common Pitfalls

- Check that the linear model is valid over the amplitudes and operating points used in the design.
- Validate noise covariances and residuals; a filter can look smooth while becoming statistically inconsistent.
