# Model Predictive Control

Repeatedly solves a finite-horizon optimization using a model of the plant.

MPC solves a finite-horizon optimization at each sample, applies the first input, then repeats with updated measurements.

A common quadratic objective is

$$
\min_{x_{0:N},u_{0:N-1}} \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} \left(\|x_k-r_k\|_Q^2 + \|u_k\|_R^2\right) + \|x_N-r_N\|_P^2
$$

subject to model dynamics

$$
x_{k+1} = f(x_k,u_k)
$$

and constraints such as

$$
x_k \in \mathcal{X}, \qquad u_k \in \mathcal{U}
$$

## Why It Matters

MPC is useful when actuator limits, state limits, previewed references, and multivariable coupling must be handled explicitly.
