# Linear state space

x_dot = A x + B u, y = C x + D u.

A continuous-time linear state-space model represents the plant through a state vector, input, and output.

$$
\dot{x}(t) = A x(t) + B u(t)
$$

$$
y(t) = C x(t) + D u(t)
$$

Here $x \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is the state, $u \in \mathbb{R}^m$ is the input, and $y \in \mathbb{R}^p$ is the measured or regulated output.

## Why It Matters

State-space form keeps internal dynamics explicit, which makes controllability, observability, observers, optimal control, and MIMO design natural.

## Controllability

A linear pair $(A,B)$ is controllable when the controllability matrix has full rank:

$$
\mathcal{C} = [B \quad AB \quad A^2B \quad \cdots \quad A^{n-1}B]
$$

## Observability

The pair $(A,C)$ is observable when

$$
\mathcal{O} = \begin{bmatrix} C \\ CA \\ CA^2 \\ \vdots \\ CA^{n-1} \end{bmatrix}
$$

has full column rank.
