# Discrete models with delays

Represent sensor, actuator, communication, and computation delays by augmentation.

## Core Idea

In control-theory terms, **Discrete models with delays** is part of the state-space models toolkit in modeling & simulation. It helps connect a model, signal, controller, estimator, or analysis method to a closed-loop objective.

A compact way to frame the objective is to minimize a performance signal while respecting the plant dynamics:

$$
J = \int_0^T \left(\|e(t)\|_Q^2 + \|u(t)\|_R^2\right) dt
$$

where $e(t)$ is the relevant tracking, estimation, or regulation error and $u(t)$ is the control input.

## Control Context

- **Model:** specify the state, input, output, uncertainty, and sampling assumptions.
- **Objective:** define the behavior that should be tracked, rejected, stabilized, estimated, or optimized.
- **Verification:** check stability, performance, constraints, and robustness before relying on the design.

## Typical Question

For this topic, the practical question is: what information is available, what behavior is required, and what tradeoff should the controller or analysis method make?
