## What It Means

**Stochastic optimal control** is a optimal topic in the **Control Methods** branch of the control-theory map. In this context, it focuses on choosing inputs by minimizing a cost functional subject to system dynamics and boundary conditions. It belongs to controller design methods that turn models, measurements, and objectives into feedback or feedforward actions, so the important question is not only what the method computes, but what assumptions make the computation meaningful for a closed loop.

A useful way to read this topic is as a connection between three objects: a model of the plant or signal, an objective stated in engineering terms, and evidence that the resulting behavior is stable, accurate, robust, or safe enough for the application.

## Mathematical Form

The continuous-time optimal-control template is

$$
\min_{u(\cdot)} J=\phi(x(T))+\int_0^T L(x(t),u(t),t)\,dt\quad\text{s.t.}\quad \dot x=f(x,u,t).
$$

The notation should be interpreted with the page topic in mind: $x$ is the state or configuration, $u$ is the manipulated input, $y$ is the measured output, $r$ is a reference, $e$ is an error signal, and disturbances or modeling errors are included when they materially affect the design.

The key intuition is to ask what information this topic adds to the feedback loop. Some topics add a model, some add a controller, some add an estimator, and some add a test.
